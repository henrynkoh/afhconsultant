### Section 1: The Problem Most AFH Owners Face

**The Medicaid Trap**

Most AFH owners start with the best intentions: they want to provide quality care to seniors. But they fall into what we call "the Medicaid trap":

- Accepting low-paying Medicaid residents ($4,000-$4,500/month)
- Working as caregivers instead of business administrators
- Using flat-rate pricing instead of tiered models
- Missing opportunities for private-pay residents ($8,500-$12,000/month)

**The Result?**

- Exhausted owners working 24/7
- Thin profit margins ($500-$1,000/month per resident)
- High staff turnover
- Burnout and business failure

**Real Numbers:**

A typical 6-bed AFH with all Medicaid residents:
- Monthly Revenue: $27,000
- Operating Costs: $22,000
- Net Profit: $5,000/month
- Owner's Draw: Minimal (owner is the primary caregiver)

Compare this to a strategically run AFH with private-pay focus:
- Monthly Revenue: $57,000
- Operating Costs: $35,000
- Net Profit: $22,000/month
- Owner's Draw: $15,000-$18,000/month

The difference? **$17,000 more profit per month**—that's $204,000 per year!

### Section 2: The Three Pillars of AFH Financial Success

After working with hundreds of AFH owners, we've identified three critical pillars that separate successful businesses from struggling ones:

#### Pillar 1: Financial Engineering & Viability

**Tiered Pricing Models**

Instead of flat-rate pricing, successful AFH owners implement tiered pricing based on level of care:

- **Level 1 (Basic Care)**: $7,500/month
- **Level 2 (Moderate Care)**: $9,500/month
- **Level 3 (High Acuity)**: $11,500/month
- **Level 4 (Intensive/Hospice)**: $13,500/month

**Add-On Services**

Smart owners bill separately for:
- Nurse Delegation: $250-$500/month
- Incontinence Management: $300-$600/month
- Two-Person Transfers: $1,500/month
- Specialty Diets: $200-$400/month

**The Daily Rate Advantage**

Using daily rates instead of monthly rates ensures you get paid for the 31st day:
- Monthly Rate: $8,500
- Daily Rate: ($8,500 × 12) ÷ 365 = $279.45/day
- Extra Revenue: $8,500/year per resident

#### Pillar 2: Medication System Integrity (WAC 388-76-10430)

Compliance isn't just about avoiding citations—it's about protecting your license and your residents.

**Common Violations:**
- Ditto marks on Medication Administration Records (MAR)
- Missing signature keys
- Improper narcotic storage
- Incomplete documentation

**The Cost of Non-Compliance:**
- State citations: $500-$5,000 per violation
- License suspension: Potential business closure
- Legal liability: Lawsuits and settlements

**The Solution:**
A "strong medication system" that includes:
- Complete documentation
- Proper storage protocols
- Staff training
- Regular audits

#### Pillar 3: Resident Rights & Disclosures (WAC 388-10510+)

Complete disclosure packets protect both residents and owners.

**Required Documents:**
- Notice of Rights and Services
- Disclosure of Services (DSHS 10-508)
- Disclosure of Charges (DSHS 15-449)
- Medicaid Conversion Policy (14-point font)

**Missing Disclosures = Immediate Citations**
